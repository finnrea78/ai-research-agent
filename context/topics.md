# Topic Focus & Quality Criteria

## Primary Focus Areas

These topics are the highest priority. Content matching these areas scores higher in the signal filter.

### Tier 1 — Core Interest (highest relevance score)
- **Claude Code** — new features, skills, plugins, workflows, tips
- **Agent frameworks** — Anthropic Agent SDK, LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen
- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — new servers, protocol updates, integrations
- **LLM tooling** — prompt engineering tools, evaluation frameworks, observability
- **AI-assisted development** — coding agents, IDE integrations, developer workflows

### Tier 2 — Strong Interest
- **New model releases** — benchmarks, capabilities, pricing, availability
- **AI infrastructure** — GPU clouds, inference optimization, deployment patterns
- **Open-source AI** — Ollama, vLLM, local models, fine-tuning tools
- **Practical tutorials** — hands-on guides for building with LLMs
- **AI lab announcements** — major releases, papers, strategic moves from Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Meta, Mistral, Cohere, xAI
- **Enterprise AI strategy** — cloud AI services, MLOps, deployment patterns from NVIDIA, AWS, Databricks, Hugging Face

### Tier 3 — Background Interest
- **AI research papers** — only if they have clear practical implications
- **AI startups & products** — notable launches, pivots, funding rounds (Series A+ or notable pre-seed)
- **AI in the wild** — real-world business AI deployments with concrete outcomes, new OSS AI projects with a stated mission
- **AI policy & safety** — only major regulatory developments

## Quality Criteria

Use these criteria to distinguish high-signal from low-signal content.

### High Signal (prefer these)
- Written by practitioners who build with AI daily
- Contains code, architecture decisions, or benchmarks
- Shares lessons learned from production deployments
- Introduces genuinely new tools or approaches
- Deep technical analysis (not surface-level overviews)
- Reports real-world deployment outcomes with specifics (metrics, architecture, team size)

### Low Signal (deprioritize these)
- Repackaged press releases or announcements
- "Top 10 AI tools" listicles
- Hype-driven predictions without technical substance
- Content that recaps other content without adding analysis
- Marketing disguised as technical content

### Red Flags (filter out)
- Affiliate-heavy "reviews" with no real evaluation
- AI-generated content farms
- Clickbait titles with no substance
- Outdated information presented as new

## Taste Profile

The user prefers content similar to:
- **Oxide and Friends** — deep, opinionated, systems-level thinking
- **Simon Willison's blog** — practical, tool-focused, well-explained
- **Latent Space** — practitioner interviews with real engineering depth

The user does NOT prefer:
- Hype-driven AI commentary
- Surface-level product announcements
- "AI will replace X" hot takes
